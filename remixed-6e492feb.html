The Problem

Preliminary layout concentrates decades of
judgment into a few days of manual work.

2–3 days

Per project, every project

Senior engineers stare at architectural drawings and use experience to decide where columns go, how beams connect them, and what slab system fits the spans. This happens on every new job. The work looks simple — it’s just placing elements on a plan — but wrong column placement creates expensive transfer structures; wrong beam direction creates inefficient load paths; wrong slab system wastes floor-to-ceiling height.

∞ redos

Every time the architect changes the plan

One revision and the layout is invalid. The engineer redoes column grid, beam arrangement, and slab choice from scratch. Firms turn down work or miss deadlines because layout is the bottleneck. The best people are stuck producing, not deciding.

Years

Before juniors can do it well

Layout requires judgment that takes years to develop. Junior engineers cannot do it well without supervision. So senior engineers either do it themselves or spend hours reviewing and correcting. There is no way to scale that experience.

0

Intellectual challenge in the task

The best engineering graduates leave the profession because their days are consumed by mechanical layout work. The actual decisions — the ones that need human expertise — get buried under the grind of producing the starting point.

The Product

What Civil Agent does

Civil Agent is an AI that generates preliminary structural layouts from architectural floor plans. You upload an IFC file. It produces column positions, beam arrangement, and slab system recommendation — the same spatial decisions a senior engineer would make, in minutes. You review, adjust, and approve. Then export directly to SAP2000 or ETABS. No manual layout from scratch.

Vision

Then load takeoff & load paths
